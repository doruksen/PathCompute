Taxonomy of Solution Approaches

Explore the dominant families of solutions addressing WSI bottlenecks. Click the tabs to toggle between Data Engineering, Model Paradigms, and System Optimization.

üìÇ Multi-Resolution Pyramids

Native WSI formats (SVS, OME-TIFF, DICOM) store images as a pyramid of resolutions. Efficient AI readers (like OpenSlide) access only the necessary magnification level, avoiding full-res decoding.

Key Tech: OME-TIFF, DICOM WSI, OpenSlide, LibVIPS

üß© Tiling & Caching

Breaking the WSI into fixed-size tiles (e.g., 256x256 or 512x512). Intelligent caching strategies predict next-needed tiles during streaming inference to reduce IO latency.

Bottleneck Solved: Memory Overflow & Latency

ü§ñ Multiple Instance Learning (MIL)

The slide is a "bag" of instances (patches). The model predicts the slide label without requiring pixel-level annotations. Methods like CLAM and Attention-MIL identify key patches automatically.

Key Papers: Ilse et al. (2018), Lu et al. (CLAM, 2021)

üï∏Ô∏è Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)

Constructs a graph where nodes are cells or tissue patches and edges represent spatial proximity. GNNs model the micro-environment topology far more efficiently than processing empty white space.

Advantage: Captures structural relationships with low compute.

üéØ ROI Selection & Cascading

Coarse-to-fine pipelines. A lightweight model scans the slide at low-res to identify Regions of Interest (ROI), then a heavy model processes only those ROIs at high-res. "Hard Mining" for patches.

Result: 90% reduction in pixel processing.

‚öôÔ∏è Mixed Precision & Distributed Training

Utilizing FP16/BF16 to halve memory usage. Using model parallelism for gigantic foundation models or data parallelism for high-throughput batching of patches.

Hardware: NVIDIA AMP, TPU Sharding.

The Trade-off Landscape

There is no "free lunch." High pixel-level accuracy usually comes at the cost of immense compute or annotation burden.

Research Gaps & Open Problems

End-to-End Training

Gradient checkpointing across 10k+ patches remains computationally prohibitive for true end-to-end backprop.

Scanner Variability

Efficient domain adaptation is needed. Color normalization adds compute overhead; can models be invariant by design?

Multimodal Integration

Efficiently fusing WSI (Gigapixel) with Genomics (1D vector) and Radiology (3D vol) without memory explosion.

Standardized Benchmarks

Community needs standard metrics for "Throughput (slides/hr)" alongside AUC, on standardized hardware.
